Getting There

The initial dataset consisted of 10,000 reviews, located as a package on Kaggle.com (see Source section for details). The dataset was scrubbed for duplicate data, reducing the data down to 9787 reviews. To improve clustering, a threshold of greater than 10 reviews was implemented, reducing review count to 8136.

Number of Reviews associated by city within threshold (10+ reviews)

Once implemented, the hotels were further grouped by their city/state association, reducing results to 64 cities.

K-means clustering was set to generate 25 different clusters. Overall, there were 10952 unique words to parse through, even after stemming and tokenization. Using the "sklearn" module, cluster analysis was completed and within those clusters, the top 10 most common words were taken.

Complications

Why choose 25 clusters as the cluster K value?


The "Elbow method", a method of analyzing the variance-squared for each series of K-clusters was attempted to be applied, but there was no satisfying result within the testing range of 1 through 100 K-clusters. This could have been expanded, but the calculation took well over 3 hours to run. In the interest of time, 25 was chosen.

What Could Have Been Done Better

During the pre-processing stage and subsequent clustering, some words appear truncated. Either the stemming process or the tokenizer is consistently transforming words incorrectly. Further experimentation will alleviate this.

As the keywords are generated from the top 10 most common words within each cluster, experimenting with different levels of word output and testing their results would allow for optimization.
